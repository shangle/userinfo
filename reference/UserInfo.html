2Check whether you are online.

3Close and reopen the browser.

Website will not load, looks broken, or keeps spinning
  1. Make sure this page says you are online.
  2. Close the browser tab and open the banking site again.
  3. If it still looks wrong, clear your browser cache using the matching section below.
  4. Try again after fully closing and reopening the browser.
  5. If support asks, copy or email the details from the buttons above.
Website keeps signing me out or forgetting me
  1. Make sure cookies are enabled in your browser.
  2. Check the section below about making sure your browser is not automatically clearing cookies or browsing history.
  3. Avoid using private or incognito browsing if you want the site to remember you.
  4. Close the browser and open it again after changing settings.
Buttons do not respond or sign-in acts strangely
  1. Some banking pages need cookies and saved site data to work correctly.
  2. Make sure you are using a current version of a major browser.
  3. If the page still behaves oddly, clear the cache and try again.
  4. If you have browser add-ons or blockers installed, support may ask you to temporarily disable them.
How to clear browser cache

If a website looks wrong, will not load properly, or keeps showing an old version, clearing the cache can help.

Google Chrome
  1. Open Chrome.
  2. Press Ctrl + Shift + Delete on Windows, or Command + Shift + Delete on Mac.
  3. Choose a time range. If unsure, choose All time.
  4. Check Cached images and files.
  5. You may leave Cookies unchecked unless support asks you to clear them too.
  6. Click Clear data.
  7. Close the browser and open it again.
Microsoft Edge
  1. Open Edge.
  2. Press Ctrl + Shift + Delete.
  3. Choose a time range. If unsure, choose All time.
  4. Check Cached images and files.
  5. Leave Cookies and other site data unchecked unless support asks for that too.
  6. Click Clear now.
  7. Close Edge and open it again.
Mozilla Firefox
  1. Open Firefox.
  2. Press Ctrl + Shift + Delete on Windows, or Command + Shift + Delete on Mac.
  3. Set the time range. If unsure, choose everything available.
  4. Check Cache.
  5. Uncheck other boxes unless support asks you to clear them too.
  6. Click Clear or OK.
  7. Close Firefox and reopen it.
Safari on Mac
  1. Open Safari.
  2. At the top of the screen, click Safari, then Settings or Preferences.
  3. Open the Advanced tab and turn on Show Develop menu in menu bar if you do not already see Develop.
  4. At the top of the screen, click Develop.
  5. Click Empty Caches.
  6. Close Safari and open it again.
Safari on iPhone or iPad
  1. Open the Settings app on your device.
  2. Scroll down and tap Apps, then Safari on newer iPhones/iPads, or just tap Safari on some versions.
  3. Tap Clear History and Website Data.
  4. Confirm if asked.
  5. Open Safari again and try the website.
How to make sure your browser is not automatically clearing cookies or history

If you keep getting signed out, websites forget you, or your browsing history disappears, these settings may be turned on.

Google Chrome
  1. Open Chrome.
  2. Click the three dots in the top-right corner.
  3. Click Settings.
  4. Click Privacy and security.
  5. Click Third-party cookies or Cookies and other site data.
  6. Make sure you do not have a setting turned on that clears cookies when Chrome closes.
  7. Go back and click Clear browsing data only when you want to clear things manually.
  8. If you use an extension that says it cleans privacy or browsing data, consider turning it off and ask support for help.
Microsoft Edge
  1. Open Edge.
  2. Click the three dots in the top-right corner.
  3. Click Settings.
  4. Click Privacy, search, and services.
  5. Scroll to Clear browsing data.
  6. Click Choose what to clear every time you close the browser.
  7. Make sure the switches for Cookies and other site data and Browsing history are turned off unless you intentionally want them cleared every time.
  8. Close Settings when finished.
Mozilla Firefox
  1. Open Firefox.
  2. Click the three lines in the top-right corner.
  3. Click Settings.
  4. Click Privacy & Security.
  5. Scroll to the History section.
  6. If Firefox says it will use custom settings for history, look for options that say Clear history when Firefox closes.
  7. Make sure that setting is unchecked if you do not want history cleared every time.
  8. Also look for cookie settings that remove cookies when Firefox closes, and turn those off if needed.
Safari on Mac
  1. Open Safari.
  2. Click Safari at the top of the screen, then Settings or Preferences.
  3. Open the Privacy tab.
  4. Review cookie and website tracking settings.
  5. If websites keep forgetting you, ask support whether your privacy settings are too strict for the site you are using.
  6. If you use private browsing often, remember that private windows do not keep browsing history the same way normal windows do.
Safari on iPhone or iPad
  1. Open the Settings app.
  2. Tap Apps, then Safari on newer versions, or tap Safari directly on some versions.
  3. Look for privacy settings and website data settings.
  4. If you are having trouble staying signed in, ask support whether any of these settings should be adjusted.
  5. If you use private browsing, remember that it is designed to keep less history.
Simple sign something may be clearing cookies: A website keeps forgetting you or makes you sign in every time you close the browser.
Simple sign something may be clearing history: You visit a website, then later it does not appear in your browser history.

What this means

Your browser is the program you use to open websites, such as Chrome, Edge, Safari, or Firefox.
Your operating system is the main software that runs your computer or device, such as Windows, macOS, Android, or iPhone/iPad.
Cookies help websites remember you. If cookies keep getting deleted, you may be signed out often or websites may forget your settings.
Cache is saved website data that helps pages load faster. Sometimes clearing it fixes website problems.
Screen size helps support understand how a website may appear on your device.
